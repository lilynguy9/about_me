At the Applied Physics Laboratory (UW) under Dr. C. Chris Chickadel and Dr. Christopher Bassett, I investigated the thermal signatures of cooling residual foam and its relationship to bubble formation and wave energy dissipation in field-scale waves, using remote sensing techniques.

Marine Renewable Energy

At the National Renewable Energy Laboratory, I supported the Water Power Technologies Office through numerical modeling and techno-economic analysis of marine renewable energy systems. My work included modifying MATLAB/SIMULINK code to integrate advanced features into a wave energy converter (WEC) simulation tool, such as dynamic wave loading on Morison elements. I also conducted cost modeling and feasibility studies on undersea pumped energy storage, and contributed to infrastructure assessments for future marine energy deployment. This experience strengthened my skills in system modeling, code development, and strategic evaluation of ocean energy technologies.

Ocean Health

Working under Dr. Joleah Lamb (UCI), I conducted data analysis near a coastal development in Myanmar where there was presence of polluting seafood with harmful bacteria and microplastics. Oysters had the highest contamination, showing how urban runoff and waste are entering the food chain and posing risks to both human health and marine ecosystems.

Littman RA, Fiorenza EA, Wenger AS, Berry KLE, van de Water JAJM, Nguyen L, Aung ST, Parker DM, Rader DN, Harvell CD, Lamb JB. Coastal urbanization influences human pathogens and microdebris contamination in seafood. Sci Total Environ. 2020 Sep 20;736:139081. doi: 10.1016/j.scitotenv.2020.139081. Epub 2020 May 5. PMID: 32504866.
